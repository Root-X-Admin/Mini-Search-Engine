The Semiconductor Revolution: What Comes After Silicon?

As traditional silicon chips approach physical limits, 2024 has seen accelerated development of alternative semiconductor technologies to power future computing needs.

Emerging Technologies:
- 2nm and smaller process nodes entering mass production
- Chiplet architectures improving yield and performance
- Gallium nitride (GaN) and silicon carbide (SiC) for power electronics
- Optical computing prototypes showing promise
- Memristor-based neuromorphic chips for AI workloads

Industry Impact:
- Dramatic improvements in energy efficiency
- Specialized processors for AI/ML workloads
- Renewed focus on domestic manufacturing capabilities
- Advanced packaging techniques extending Moore's Law

These developments are critical for meeting demands in AI, autonomous systems, and the Internet of Things while addressing global chip supply chain challenges.
